What is the Digital Humanities Research Institute at Southern Methodist University?

The Digital Humanities Research Institute (DHRI) at SMU is a workshop series to be held in August 19-22, 2019 at Fondren Library, SMU.

Application information here


Applications open: Applications received by April 15th receive priority consideration. Applicants will hear back by May 15th.

Reasons to Participate

Build Community

Engage with leaders who are passionate about using digital tools to enhance their work.

Learn New Skills

Build technical and professional skills through collaborative and group-oriented workshops

Share Resources

Learn how to teach, adapt, and share our open curricula with others.

What to Expect

The goal for DHRI@SMU is to create an intensive, community-oriented, and foundational approach to learning technical skills in service of humanities teaching and learning.


About Us

DHRI at SMU is a pilot program that is working towards building and sustaining an active community around the shared idea of Digital Humanities and Digital Scholarship.
